Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. It usually occurs when 
fraudsters trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fraudulent payment requests.
In a typical scenario, a victim receives a call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official 
or customer support agent. The fraudster claims that there is an issue with the user’s account or offers a 
reward or cashback. 
They then send a fake payment request or ask the victim to enter their UPI PIN on a malicious link or app.
Once the victim enters their UPI PIN or approves the request, money is instantly transferred from their 
account to the fraudster’s account. Students and elderly people are often targeted because they may not be 
fully aware of such scams.
The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in digital payment systems. 
In many cases, victims are unable to recover their money if they do not report the fraud immediately.
